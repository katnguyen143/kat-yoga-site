welcome

hi, i'm kat!

I’m a yoga teacher and acrobat from the Bay Area currently based in Koh Phangan, Thailand, where I teach at Nestra Beach Shala — a community-centered space offering free yoga and English classes to Thai and Burmese locals.

In 2024, I began teaching yoga with Outsiders, an outdoor rec club at UC Berkeley where I led free weekly classes for BIPOC students. After completing my 200-hour training at Bali Yoga Ashram in June 2025, I’ve continued teaching yoga in Japan and Thailand, offering everything from calming yang-to-yin sunset flows to energizing handstand and inversion practices. I also teach university yoga classes, lead sessions for friends in parks and tatami rooms, and share movement in spaces that value accessibility and connection.

Yoga is a practice of breath, presence, and community, and everyone deserves access to it. Whether you’re stretching for the first time or flying through the air, I’m here to support your journey. ♡

current offerings

yoga and acroyoga classes

group yoga classes

My group classes are designed to be welcoming, dynamic, and accessible to all levels. Whether you're brand new to yoga or looking to deepen your practice, you'll be supported with clear guidance, breath cues, and optional props. Expect a mix of strength-building movement, breathwork, and space to move at your own pace.

donation-based yoga classes

Yoga was never meant to be a privilege, yet financial barriers often make it feel that way. These classes are offered on a donation basis to make yoga more accessible, especially for BIPOC and local communities. All levels are welcome. Pay what you can, and know that your presence is enough.

private acroyoga classes

Learn the foundations of acro or dive into flows, whips, pops, and hand-to-hand. As a seasoned flyer, I specialize in helping bases build strength, balance, and clear communication. Whether you're just starting out or refining your technique, we’ll co-create trust and flow at your own pace.

reserve a class

For island classes at Nestra Beach Shala, please arrive at least 10 minutes early to settle in. Yoga is best practiced on an empty stomach, so we recommend avoiding food for about two hours beforehand to support ease and comfort in your practice.

  • Group yoga classes — cash on arrival or card online (Calendly → PayPal)
  • Donation-based options are available. No one turned away for lack of funds
  • Acroyoga jams and workshops — follow kat.acro for updates
